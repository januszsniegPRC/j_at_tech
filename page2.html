1947

Invention of the revolutionary transistor, John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley.

1960

Military were the first to start using digital computers, American Airlines Sabre flight reservation system processed 84,000 telephone calls a day, storing 807 megabytes of reservations and flight schedules which back then was on a large hard drive alternative.

1962

The term database is first mentioned in Oxford dictionaries.

1963

Charles Bachman develops the Integrated Data Store (IDS) an ever first navigational database model in Manufacturing Information and Control System (MIACS).

1964

One of the commonly known programming languages introduced, BASIC.

1965

Gordon Moore work “Cramming more components onto integrated circuits” widely known as Moore’s Law - observation of crammed transistors in a dense unified circuit, doubling every two years, reducing the price and increased output power of electronic parts.

1968-69

Machine Readable Cataloging records and Willard Boyle and George E. Smith at AT&T Bell Labs forge charge-coupled device translating light into electric signals revolutionising digital camera industry, 40 years later they were awarded with a Nobel Prize in Physics.

Arpanet (advanced research of projects agency network) is released commonly used only by the military. It was a pre-successor to the internet we know today.

1970

Edgar F. Codd work “A relational model of data for large shared data banks”, proposing a theoretical thesis leading to the main type of database.

1971

Arthur Miller, The Assault on Privacy, “Too many information handlers seem to measure a man by the number of bits of storage capacity his dossier will occupy.” and Mchael Hart goes with the “Project Gutenberg” to provide copyright-free digitally available using U.S. Declaration of independence.

1972-73

Pulsar the world’s first watch to use digital numericals to display time, Charles Bachman is awarded with the Turing Award on The Programmer as Navigator and Court ruling declaring John V. Atanasoff and Clifford E. Berry were the first digital computer inventors whilst tracing back from the Electronic Numerical Integrator and Calculator (ENIAC)

1975

Steven Sasson’s digital camera takes 23 seconds to capture first image, the input was stored on a cassette.

1977

“The Citi never sleeps” campaign ad, Citibank initiates their first ATM that operated 24/7 and only one normally failed.

1979

Federal Express turns COSMOS (Customers, Operations, and Services Master Online System) it was a management system on people or packages e.g. it had a storage of 80 gigabytes.

1980

Enhanced fiber optics enabled to further develop communications across computers, analog signals was replaced by the digital signals particularly in telephone routes, pulse code modulation (PCM) changes analog data into digital data compared to analog they were less distorted and very easy to duplicate.

1981

Edgar F. Codd is awarded with a Turing Award for continuous help in development of database management in ATM’s.

1982

A film “Bladerunner” predicts how year 2019 will look like from advanced robotics that look like humans, flying cars and holographic displays, the first commercial compact disc is produced.

1983

Early days of the internet we know today, when arpanet starts using the internet protocol. Internet protocol goes through stages such as UDP or data.

1984

Apple Macintosh is released to the public and Sony becomes the first company that aims to replace floppy discs with their introduction of computer disks.

1985

First virtual society is developed in a system named: Whole Earth’s ‘Lectronic Link a.k.a. WELL.

1986

EMAP is being defined for email transfer.

1988

Commercial compact discs dominate the industry overtaking vinyls in sells. Cornell students launched his worm virus.

1989

15% American households had computers in their possession. Very first release of the Microsoft Office, by Microsoft.

1990

Successful conversion of analog HDTV into a digital HDTV. 0.05% people in the world are using internet.

1991

First 2G Cellular network was launched in Finland, it preferred digital communicative methods rather than analog in cell phones. This meant that text messaging was available. 1 million people registered to be using internet. It becomes demilitarized essentially and available for public use.

CERN’s web browser released for public use.

1992

Tim Berners-Lee uploads first digital picture onto a website showcasing the all-female parody group “Les Horribles Cernettes”.

1993

Era of new advertisement begins, O’Reilly Digital Media group starts off with Global Network Navigator which offered first clickable advertisements. Intel Pentium is introduced, this processor will revolutionise home-use computers.

1994

Teradata becomes the largest cooperation so far in terms of data capacity resulting in 10 terabytes. A pepperoni pizza is first ordered online from PizzaHut. Jeff Bezos goes off with his idea of Amazon, at the beginning it was just a simple book store but in 1996 it expanded its product line.

‘Smart’-phone released which supports calls, email and fax.

1995

Archival materials from the Library of Congress are becoming digitalized. Norweagian Broadcasting Corporation launches the first digital radio. A classic cartoon “Toy Story” goes live in cinemas which was created fully using computer-generated imagery (CGI).

Invention of DVD (digital versatile disc).

Paper becomes the worse alternative option compared to digital storage and with E-gold launching it became widely first known digital currency.

1998

Turning Award lands in Jim Gray’s hands for contributing to database and transaction processing research and system utilization. Analog cameras sale peaks at 40 million before being replaced by digital cameras. Digital Television is being launched in the UK, analog signal was converted and replaced television output in digital signal. Microsoft masters the binary system of 1’s and 0’s. The Last Broadcast is the first movie to be digitally shot, edited and distributed and available to download.

Commercial digital television was broadened internationally, communication satellites were developed to transmit large quantities of data to thousands of households receiving multiple choices of your favourite channels
